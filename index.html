Introduction

No one in the world is not familiar with the word "Friend". Almost everyone is aware of this word and its importance in life. The word carries a fragrance in itself. The friend is like the base of a house who is present in life and makes a house beautiful. We don't compare to the moments when we are with our friends, the moments may be in long drives, holidays, summer vacations, or group studies. We believe in our friends too because we know that whenever we are in any problem or crisis, our friends are present there without selflessness. They are important in our lives and Hinduism's "Lord Krishna and Sudama" friendship is an example of a true and selfless story, which tells us the importance of friends. In our childhood, we choose our friends who play with us, we grow together, we study, and we enjoy every moment together. It is a relation whose experience varies for everyone, but a common factor is its impact on life is wordless. Nowadays, friends have become more valuable and true friends are rare. A good statement is delivered by the philosopher Plato.

“True friendship can exist only between equals.” ― Plato

The First Meeting

I clearly remember the day, that was Monday in school. We met in the morning during the period of Science, my age was sixteen. It was our first meeting. He was sitting on a bench I noticed that day. He forgot the ID of the school. I went near him and told him "Are you Manu?" This was my first sentence about our friendship. Then we spent a lot of time with each other. In school, in coaching classes, on the ground, in swimming classes, everywhere we both went together. That time was very good for us. He provided me with notes when I was absent from the classroom or coaching. He cared for my plants very well in my absence which we planted on the school campus, on the side of the botanical garden. I remember how he helped me one day when I had forgotten my purse at home and I needed Rs.500 urgently for the submission of annual fees. At that time it was the last day of the submission fee. He asked me how he could help. Initially, I ignored the issue but due to his persistence, I told him the story. Within an hour, he managed the money for me. I was unaware of how he managed it and from whom, but he did that. From that day on, our friendship bond became stronger day by day.

The Qualities of My Friend

He has many qualities that make him a humble and attractive man. Some of the qualities are:

  • Nature Lover: He is always down to earth. He belongs to a rich family but his attitude is always helpful to others, and he is a very grounded man who never forgets his roots and culture. Once, I was with him on a summer vacation when we went to his village. When I asked him, "You have proper money, why don’t you spend your vacations at a hill station?", he simply replied, "I can go, but I don’t want to. My ancestors were from the village." This was not the only instance that showed his nature, but it was evident in his clothing, walking style, and conversation. He doesn’t use social media, which tells of his simple nature.
  • Passion for Hobbies: My friend is very punctual. Even though sometimes I forget the time for any work, he doesn’t. His passion for his hobbies is intense. Near his house, there is a club famous in the area for kabaddi tournaments and practice. Every Sunday, he goes to the club and starts his kabaddi practice. I never understood why he was so interested in this game. His dedication to the game never waned. Once, he received bad grades in an exam and needed to make more effort in a particular subject to improve his grades. The next Sunday, he skipped his studies and went to the club again.
  • Gratitude: One thing that makes him different or interesting is his gratitude. This trait is very rare, and people often forget about the things or blessings they have received. Once, our whole class went outside for a picnic. He found a broken watch there that was not so beautiful or high-quality. He took it and asked every student who was present there if it belonged to them. The next day, he asked everyone again, but no one was interested in the watch. When I told him to throw it away, he didn’t react. The next day, the watch was strapped to his left hand.

How Did He Make an Impact on Me?

My friend Manu made a significant impact on my life. Because of him, in childhood, I spent a lot of time with a person who was a deep thinker, attached to culture, had no arrogance, and was always punctual. These qualities provided the foundation for my life, reflecting positivity in my actions. Whenever I face any issue or situation, he always stands by me, offering support and guidance.

Conclusion

We all choose friends in our lives, and they are important because they help us and make an impact on us—often a timeless impact. They improve our quality of life. However, not all friends are good or well-intentioned. Some are selfish and put their preferences first in any situation. Nowadays, good friends are rare to find. Some people ruin their friend's lives by introducing them to harmful habits like alcoholism or other addictions for their own benefit. We need to be aware of this type of friend. A good friend always keeps their friendship a priority and will guide their friend towards making good decisions in life. In contrast, bad friends never support their friends; they only use them. After they fulfill their needs, they abandon them. It is important to give our time and effort to a good friend who improves both lives.
